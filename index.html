About

I am a Research Associate in the Department of Computer Science & Engineering at the University of Minnesota. My work spans autonomous vehicles, teleoperation, multi-sensor perception, robotics, and network-aware intelligent systems. I am particularly interested in practical deployment challenges, including latency, bandwidth constraints, human factors, and robustness in real-world environments.

Research Themes

My research aims to bridge advanced autonomy with real-world constraints by combining perception, reasoning, networking, and human oversight.

Remote Driving & Teleoperation

Adaptive video streaming, operator situational awareness, control responsiveness, and safe human intervention under network uncertainty.

Collaborative Perception

Bandwidth-aware AV-edge cooperation, selective feature sharing, and perception enhancement for autonomous and remote operation.

Multimodal Reasoning

Vision-language reasoning for escalation decisions, scene understanding, and context-aware robotic assistance.

Selected Projects

Scalable Remote Driving Systems

Research on adaptive video streaming and human-factor requirements for real-world teleoperation deployment under variable wireless conditions.

Rural Road Safety with C-V2X

Integrated communications, sensing, and simulation framework for improving safety at rural intersections with realistic network measurements.

VLM-Driven Intervention and Escalation

Developing semantically grounded reasoning systems to determine when autonomous systems should request human assistance.

AV-Edge Collaborative Perception

Designing adaptive feature and data-sharing strategies to improve operator awareness and perception robustness under bandwidth limitations.
